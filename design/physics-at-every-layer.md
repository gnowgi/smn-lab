# Physics at every layer — the scale-recursive CAZ and conservation-typed opponency

*A theory note enriching the SMN architecture (GN, 2026-07). It does not disturb the
Phase I programme; it deepens why that programme is right, and gives the physicists a
complex-systems reason to engage. Feeds the preprint's architecture section and the
Phase II evo-devo rung.*

## Thesis

Physics does not sit underneath cognition as a substrate to be abstracted away — it
**enters at every layer**, and it is what *enables* cognition. Enactively, cognition
is the **transformation of a multi-layered topology that conserves geometry**. The
SMN stays exactly as it is; it gains a vertical axis (scale) and a physical
regulator (a conservation law) at each level.

## 1. The CAZ is scale-recursive

The CAZ we have been modelling — an opponent, elastic, (volume-)conserving unit with
embedded transducers — is not tied to one scale. The *same* model recurs down and up:

| Level | The CAZ at this level | Opponency / conservation |
|---|---|---|
| molecular | actin–myosin cross-bridge network | contraction vs elastic restoring; volume of the filament lattice |
| **cell** (myofibril / sarcomere) | an elastic, **volume-conserving** contractile unit whose shape = the protein network's elastic state | volume conservation |
| receptor | the **muscle spindle** — itself such a unit, regulated by volume *or* force conservation (§2) | field-contingent |
| tissue | a muscle = a chain of these (what the [lattice](../docs/experiments/lattice_self_model.md) models) | longitudinal / circular |
| organ | hydrostat (tongue, gut) or lever (limb) | volume vs force (§2) |
| organism | the body plan | nested |

This is a **finite, self-similar hierarchy** — scale-recursive, renormalization in
flavour, not literally fractal-to-infinity. The payoff for the bench is direct: we
already showed the self-model read-out is **topology-invariant** (chain / tree /
sheet / tube, one function). The scale-recursion says it is also **scale-invariant** —
the same `coupling` recovers the graph of a myofibril lattice, a muscle lattice, and
a body lattice. *One function, any body, any level.*

## 2. Opponency is conservation-typed, and the type is field-contingent

A CAZ's regulation is a **conservation law**, and there are two, set by the physical
field the agent is situated in:

- **Volume conservation** — the incompressible / **hydrostatic** mode. Contract one
  axis and another is *forced* to expand. This is the intrinsic cellular mode and the
  evolutionarily **ancient aquatic** regime (buoyant, no lever needed).
- **Force / torque conservation** — the **balance** mode. An opponent pair holds
  mechanical equilibrium about a lever against a field. This is the **gravitational /
  skeletal** regime, and it splits further into **exo-** and **endo-**skeletal.

Which law governs a given CAZ depends on *where the agent is* — aquatic vs
gravitational. This is not a taxonomy of organisms; it is a **regulatory choice made
per CAZ per field**, and it maps onto a distinction already latent in the bench:

- the crawler's hinge + flexor/extensor pair = **force-conserving** lever (skeletal);
- the [lattice](../docs/experiments/lattice_self_model.md)'s overdamped spring-tendon
  tissue = the **soft, (toward-)volume-conserving** regime;
- true volume conservation (the aquatic hydrostat) = the **FLEX** upgrade already
  flagged in [sheets-and-tubes](sheets-and-tubes.md).

## 3. The ancient layer nests — it does not vanish

Evolution does not replace the aquatic/volume-conserving architecture when it adds
gravitational/force-conserving structure; it **layers the new on the old**. A
vertebrate limb is force-conserving levers built *on top of* volume-conserving
cellular hydrostats built *on top of* osmotically-regulated membranes. Every real
agent is therefore **aquatic-inside-terrestrial** — a nested stack of conservation
regimes. This is precisely the [Phase II](../docs/phase2.md) evo-devo ladder
(polarized → tubular → segmented → bilateral → appendicular) re-read *vertically*:
each rung is a new conservation layer wrapping the older ones.

## 4. Opponency at the origin — the osmotic membrane

Push the CAZ down to life's beginning and it is still there. Liquid water — an
aquatic medium — is a state of matter possible only under a **gravitational field**
strong enough to hold it. The **cell membrane** is the most basal CAZ: it resists
both the physical field and particle **diffusion**, by localizing large particles
inside and regulating solute concentration through **osmosis**. Osmotic regulation is
opponency — inward vs outward pressure held in balance. So opponency is not a late
motor trick; it is there at the first partition of inside from outside.

## 5. The canvas principle — deep layers are ground, not figure

Deeper layers are **less perturbed** than outer ones: they are the stable **canvas**
on which the active top layers **write**. So the self-model need not sharply
re-encode them — a *faint* deep readout is a **feature, not a bug** (a sharp one
would be a canvas that keeps redrawing itself). Demonstrated on the
[nested lattice](../docs/experiments/nested_lattice_self_model.md): across every
perturbation regime, the active (coarse-grained) levels recover at 1.00 while the
deep level stays faint — *the self-model of the writing does not depend on the
fidelity of the canvas*. This is the reafference cut one level up (canvas = predicted
baseline, writing = residual event) and the geometry-conserving view made mechanical:
the deep canvas **conserves** the reference geometry, the top layers **transform** it.

## 6. The neural anchor — sensitivity scales with nested depth

The cortical **homunculus** magnifies the **tongue and lips** enormously — and the
tongue is the paradigm **muscular hydrostat**, a deep multi-layered volume-conserving
topology. The neural substrate scales not with body-surface area but with the
**depth of nested CAZ topology**. This *extends the resolution principle*:

> resolution ∝ CAZ density × internal capacity × **depth of nesting**.

That is a testable prediction and a clean handle for the bench (Q1b already varies
density; nesting adds the vertical axis).

## 7. Emancipation, the ratchet, and the action-pattern hierarchy

The [canvas principle](#5-the-canvas-principle-deep-layers-are-ground-not-figure) has
a mechanism: **emancipation**. An emancipated part — a head freed by a neck, then
eyes, tongue, digits — can move **cheaply**, *writing on the lower layers without
displacing them*. Moving a whole limb perturbs the entire network (expensive); a
flick of the eye or a tap of a digit does not perturb the canvas but can inscribe it.
Emancipation is *why the canvas stays a canvas while the top writes*.

Emancipation is what lets an agent **climb the action-pattern hierarchy**:

\[
\text{BAP (basal)} \;\to\; \text{HAP (haltable)} \;\to\; \text{NAP (negotiable)}
\;\to\; \cdots \;\to\; \text{TAP}.
\]

Haltability is the **HAP** rung. But haltability is **necessary, not sufficient**: a
one-time halt is not yet cognitive. The sufficient unit is a haltable action
*pattern* — a **recurrent, recognisable** halt ("a differentiation of difference
mapped to a stable recurring phenomenology"). A recurrent haltable pattern against a
thing **is** object-directedness: the two **co-occur**. That is the Phase I capstone;
**TAPs** are the next-phase capstone.

**Counting is further still, and needs a ratchet.** To count, an agent must
individuate **particulars** — *this* object as distinct from a similar token (the 2nd
epistemic transition) — else it recounts the same one. Holding each discrete state
without slipping back is a **ratchet**, and the ratchet is *not* the bodily hold
alone. It has two sources:

- **bodily** — emancipated, branched, digitised appendages (the dexterity that lets a
  part mark a state cheaply, off the canvas);
- **external / social** — traces left in the world and the participation of other
  agents, which hold the memory that lets discrete states enter symbolic processing.

So: **world model (habitat adaptation) ≠ nameable registers.** The first is Phase I;
the second waits on emancipation, particulars, and the bodily-plus-social ratchet —
Phase II and M2.

## How this enters the modeling (without disturbing Phase I)

1. **Type the CAZ.** Give each CAZ two new attributes: a **scale level** and a
   **conservation law** (`volume` | `force`). Purely additive to the current model.
2. **Scale-invariance demo.** A two-level nested lattice (a lattice whose nodes are
   themselves small lattices); show the *same* `coupling` recovers the graph at both
   the fine and coarse level — the vertical companion to the horizontal
   topology-invariance result. Small, high-value, and directly on the Phase I·① claim.
3. **Conservation-duality as a variable.** Volume-conserving (FLEX hydrostat, aquatic)
   vs force-conserving (rigid lever, gravitational) — same self-model, different
   behaviour. Slots into the sheets-and-tubes FLEX-upgrade rung.
4. **Osmotic CAZ (basal rung).** A minimal membrane model: a partition holding a
   solute gradient by opponent osmotic pressure — the origin-of-opponency demo.
5. **Resolution × depth.** Extend Q1b to vary nesting depth, testing the neural-anchor
   prediction.

## Why physicists (complex systems) care

  statistical-mechanics / continuum-mechanics object.

  levels, coarse-grained.
- **Emergence of cognition** from nested opponency — cognition as geometry-conserving
  transformation of topology, which is a physically stated, falsifiable claim.

# The Construction of Experience

## Non-inertial movement as the work of living and cognizing at once

### Premise

An agent does not first live and then, as a separate luxury, cognize. In the
minimal case these are one activity. To stay in existence the agent must act on
its world; and the very acting that keeps it alive is what constructs its
experience. **Non-inertial movement is the work the agent does to live and to
cognize at the same time.** This lesson shows how the smallest body capable of
that work is built, and why building it is already a theory of how experience is
constructed.

We proceed constructively, one commitment at a time: mass, then blocks, then the
beam between them, then movement, then the transitions movement unlocks.

---

### 1. Mass — the first gift

A body segment must have mass. This is not an incidental physical fact; it is the
single property that makes a segment *present* in the world at all, and it does
**triple duty**:

1. **Gravity for free.** A massive segment is coupled to the gravitational field
   without any mounted transducer. "Down" is an ambient reference vector, handed
   to the agent by physics rather than constructed from differences.
2. **Purchase for free.** The same weight presses the segment's ventral surface
   onto the substrate, supplying the normal force — and hence the friction — that
   turns internal shape change into external displacement. Without weight there
   is no traction, and movement is impossible.
3. **Self-report.** Mass is the load that proprioception reads as the segment's
   own state.

A massless segment senses no down, grips nothing, and reports nothing. Mass is
the minimal condition for a segment to be a thing in the world — the precondition
of both living (purchase) and orienting (gravity).

---

### 2. The chain of blocks — how to build a body

The route to a body is **segmentation, not appendages.** Repeated segments
(metamerism) are the ancestral body plan; appendages are later outgrowths *of*
segments. In evo-devo order the worm precedes the spider — the spider is a worm
that grew legs.

This gives the invariant data structure for the whole bench:

> **A body is a graph of segments (blocks).**
> A chain is the minimal case. Appendages are branches off the chain. A radial or
> bilateral body is another topology of the same graph.

The *form* of this structure does not change when the *number* of blocks grows.
That invariance is exactly what we want from the common representation: the same
code describes a three-block crawler and a many-block organism; worm versus
spider is just the topology of one data structure. Adding appendages, like adding
a modality, is an elaboration on a fixed segmental baseline — not a redesign.

---

### 3. The messaging beam — the *between* that shares the state-space

Between every two blocks there is more than a mechanical joint. There is a
**messaging beam**: the informational coupling along which the blocks maintain and
share a common state-space. This is not plumbing. It is the structure that turns
a row of independent movers into a single cognitive organism.

The reason the *between* matters so much is that everything cognitive lives there,
not in the blocks:

- **Agency** lives between blocks — a single block cannot move itself; only the
  changing *relation* between two masses, braced against the world, is
  self-movement.
- **Binding** lives between blocks — the state that represents "left-anterior is
  hotter than right" is a relation across the body, held by the beam, not by any
  one segment.
- **The world model** lives between blocks — it is precisely the shared
  state-space, maintained over the beam, *insofar as it differentiates the world.*
  If the shared state-space cannot tell the world's configurations apart, there is
  no world model, however many sensors are present.

So a body is blocks (mass, sensors, actuators) **plus the beam between them**. The
beam is what we add to a chain to make it cognize.

---

### 4. Non-inertial movement — why it takes (at least) three blocks

The agent lives in a non-inertial world: drag dominates, inertia is negligible,
and **nothing coasts.** This sets a hard bar, named by Purcell's *scallop
theorem*: a body with a single degree of freedom that cycles back and forth
produces **zero** net displacement — the return stroke exactly undoes the power
stroke.

There are exactly two ways to break that time-reversal symmetry, and each defines
a minimal mover:

- **Two phased joints** — a three-block chain. The pair of joint angles traces a
  loop in shape-space that encloses area; that non-reciprocal cycle yields net
  translation even under symmetric drag.
- **One traveling bend + anisotropic drag** — an undulatory crawler in which
  lateral drag exceeds tangential drag, so a single traveling wave produces
  forward thrust. This is *C. elegans*: overdamped, segmented, undulatory — the
  minimal non-inertial crawler, and (usefully) a model organism.

The structures that produce the movement are the ones already named:

- The **opponent pair** — two pull-only actuators across a joint — is the atomic
  unit. Movement is not a command but a shift in the *balance* of an opposition;
  the CAZ modulates the tension ratio and the mechanics do the rest.
- The **messaging beam** phase-lags one joint behind its neighbor, turning two
  bends into a traveling wave. Only nearest-neighbor coupling is needed; no center.
- **Bilateral** opponent arrangement gives steering for free: bias the balance and
  the wave curves (turning as a haltable pattern).

### 5. The ladder of transitions

The single-block thought experiment organizes the whole architecture. A single
block can be *displaced* — a pancake sliding down a slope — but the world does
that work; the block contributes nothing. Displacement is not agency. From this
one rung the ladder follows:

```
 1 block    →  mass, presence, gravity-sensing.
               No self-caused change → no reafference → no self/world, no agency.
               A pure object. It can only be moved.

 2 blocks   →  one joint → the first SELF-caused change.
 (1 joint)     Reafference becomes possible → the SELF/WORLD distinction.
               But non-inertial + one DOF = scallop: it can wiggle in place,
               generating reafference, yet go NOWHERE.

 3 blocks   →  two joints → the first non-reciprocal cycle → net LOCOMOTION.
 (2 joints)    Now the body moves THROUGH the gradient field, actively samples it
               ("is it hotter as I go this way?") → ABOUTNESS becomes possible.
```

This explains the asymmetry that motivated the whole inquiry. **Self/world is
ancient and already established** because it is unlocked at *two* blocks:
self-caused change is enough, and no travel is required. **Aboutness is not yet
established** because it is unlocked only at *three*: you must be able to *go
somewhere* to sample a "where." Unlocalized sensation — pain you cannot place — is
the signature of a body below this rung.

The minimal organism is therefore three axial segments not for convenience but
because **three blocks is the first morphology at which aboutness is physically
possible.** That is the body the bench should be built on.

### 6. Mounting the sensor blocks — a construction kit

The axial chain of blocks is a *skeleton*: mass, opponent pairs, and the messaging
beam between them. It can move, but it does not yet feel much. Experience is
constructed by **mounting sensor blocks onto the segments** — building the agent
the way one builds with Lego/Logo bricks (the constructionist heritage of Papert,
Minsky, and Resnick): a body is assembled by snapping modular parts onto a frame,
and each part added gives the agent a new character.

Where each block goes is not arbitrary. Placement follows the *spatial logic of
what the modality is for.*

**Field / gradient sensors — distributed, per segment.**
A gradient is undefined on a single sample; it exists only across spatially
separated readings. So the modalities that read the *texture of the world* are
mounted on every segment:

- a **ventral tactile skin** on each segment reads contact **force** — the same
  surface that supplies purchase doubles as the force sensor;
- **bilateral strips** along each segment read **pressure, chemical, and thermal**
  variation across the body.

Distributed this way, these blocks build the **body-relative differentiation
field** — the substrate that lets the shared state-space register "left-anterior
is hotter than right." This is the route to aboutness.

**Localizing / distal sensors — paired, anterior, sparse.**
An **eye** or an **ear** exists to fix a distal source in a single privileged
frame; its localization basis is destroyed by scattering it across the body.
Mounted as a **pair on one (anterior) segment**, it does something the distributed
field cannot: it gives the agent **polarity** — a front. The localizer is what
makes one end of the chain the *anterior pole*. Adding more eyes on more segments
does not add capacity; it adds diffuse, unmodulatable input and *reduces* focus
(the resolution principle).

**Growth — dexterity by more segments and branches.**
The same construction kit scales two ways:

- **more segments** in the axial chain → more degrees of freedom, finer waves,
  richer movement along the body;
- **branches** off the axial chain → appendages, reach, and local dexterity.

A branch is just a segment attached to a segment — the segmental graph again — so
appendages are not a new kind of thing but a topology of the same kit. The worm
grows toward the spider by adding segments and branches, never by being redesigned.

The picture is now complete: an **axial skeleton** that moves, **distributed field
skins and strips** that build the differentiation field, an **anterior localizer
pair** that gives polarity, and **added segments and branches** that give
dexterity. Each mounted block adds a character to the agent; together they
construct a body that has a world.

---

### 7. The lesson

Non-inertial movement is the work the agent does to live and to cognize at the
same time. To move is to construct experience: the body must spend effort against
drag to get anywhere, and that same effort is what carries its sensing into the
world and makes a "where" available to it. The first body that can move
non-inertially through its world is the first body that *has* a world.

Building this minimal organism — blocks with mass, opponent pairs between them, a
messaging beam that shares their state — is therefore not preparation for the
science. It is the first result of it.

crawler](experiments/c0_crawler.md) (non-inertial movement and the messaging
beam) and [C1 — the crawler under gravity](experiments/c1_touch.md) (the touch
skin and objecthood as resistance).

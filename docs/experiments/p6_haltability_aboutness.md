# E3 — Haltability generates aboutness (the manipulator unit)

!!! note "Exploratory trial — P/E series"
    Part of the bench's first exploratory series, built while learning the bench:
    a **proof-of-concept**, not a clean ablation (several runs are single-seed and
    some metrics saturate). The disciplined model organism and the going-forward
    line is the **C-series** — [C0](c0_crawler.md), [C1](c1_touch.md) — grounded in
    [Lesson 1](../lesson1.md).


`experiments/p6_haltability_aboutness.py`

[E1](p4_manipulator_objecthood.md) read a thing by its resistance;
[E2](p5_self_field_object.md) separated self, field, and object. This experiment
asks the directedness question: in what sense is the agent's press *about* the
thing it presses? The claim is that a **halt-in-contact is not a stop but a
directed state** — and that this directedness, the body's minimal form of
aboutness, is generated by **haltability**, not by rhythm.

## Setup

A thing sits on the **left**; both limbs receive the **same** command. A
halt-in-contact (`contact` high, `velocity` low) is tracked on each side over an
acquire → hold → withdraw → rest cycle, and the held halt is probed for three
properties:

| property | test | signature |
|---|---|---|
| **persistent** | a perturbation torque shoves the left limb off the object mid-hold | the agent restores the directed halt |
| **returnable** | repeated withdraw → re-press cycles | the same thing is re-acquired across cycles |
| **side-specific** | the right limb gets the identical command but has nothing to press | the right limb never holds — the L-halt / R-free asymmetry *is* directedness |

The **foil** is a **CPG** (basal rhythm) driving the limb: it touches the object
every cycle but, being a rhythm, is forced to release. The dependent variable is
the **longest sustained directed halt** — the aboutness signature — plus the number
of halt episodes (returnability) and held fraction.

## What it measures

| panel | claim | shows |
|---|---|---|
| **A — SMN left vs right** | the halt is persistent, returnable, side-specific | left holds across cycles & restores after the perturbation; right never holds |
| **B — CPG foil** | rhythm is not aboutness | the object state only flickers — never a sustained held halt |
| **C — summary** | a *sustained* directed halt appears only with haltability, only on the object side | longest sustained halt, SMN-left vs SMN-right vs CPG-left |

## Result

```
agent/limb          longest halt (s)   episodes   held fraction
SMN left (object)               1.51          5          high
SMN right (none)                0.00          0          0.00
CPG left (object)               0.26         17           low

persistent: SMN sustains a 1.5s halt vs CPG's 0.3s;
returnable: re-acquired 5×;  side-specific: right longest halt 0.00s
```

- **Persistent.** The left zone sustains a directed halt for ~1.51 s and restores
  it after the perturbation knocks it off — the object plus the continued press are
  a halt equilibrium.
- **Returnable.** Across withdraw/re-press cycles the agent re-acquires the same
  thing five times — the thing's place is a returnable possibility of modulation.
- **Side-specific.** The right zone, given the identical command but nothing to
  press, never holds (0.00 s). The left-halt / right-free asymmetry *is*
  directedness toward the object.
- **The CPG foil only flickers.** Its longest sustained halt is ~0.26 s (seventeen
  brief touches), because a rhythm is forced to release. Rhythm is not aboutness;
  haltability is.

## What this experiment shows

- **Aboutness is a sustained, directed halt.** Being *about* a thing, at this floor,
  is holding a committed halt-in-contact — persistent under perturbation,
  returnable across cycles, and specific to the side the thing is on.
- **It is haltability, not rhythm.** A basal rhythm can touch but cannot hold; only
  the haltable layer can commit the body to the thing.
- **The bilateral body makes directedness measurable.** With both limbs commanded
  identically, the asymmetry between the object side and the empty side isolates
  directedness from mere activity.

It does **not** yet show:

- **A free-space stiffness equilibrium.** Here the object supplies the equilibrium
  and the press restores it; a co-contracted equilibrium with tunable stiffness in
  *free* space needs muscle/tendon actuators (ideal torque motors make
  co-contraction inert) — a later refinement.

## What it adds to the assumptions

[Common assumptions](../assumptions.md) hold, plus:

- A **bilateral** two-limb unit with both limbs commanded identically, so the
  object-side / empty-side asymmetry is the only difference between them.
- A **perturbation probe**: an applied torque during the hold that shoves the limb
  off the object, to test whether the directed halt is restored.
- A **CPG foil** (basal rhythmic drive) on the same body, as the no-haltability
  comparison.

## Why this experiment is in the bench

It supplies the third leg of the single-zone account — directedness — without which
the difference register of E1 would be a sensor reading, not a stance toward a
thing. Aboutness grounded in haltability is the bench's bridge from the unmediated
agent (M1) toward the *about*-ness that mediated cognition (M2) will trade in.

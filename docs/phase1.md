# Phase I — grounding self, world & object in physics

Cognitive science uses words — *self*, *world*, *object*, *aboutness* — as if their
meaning were given. Phase I of smn-lab asks the physicist's question instead: **what
would it take, in a physical body, for each of these tokens to acquire a specific,
measurable meaning?** The claim under test is that they are not primitives to be
assumed but constructions to be *earned* — by a moving, sensing body whose zones
(CAZ) modulate their own single-interface transducers. That is why physics +
cognition is not a slogan here: the meaning of a cognitive token is fixed by the
physics that constructs it.

Phase I grounds three tokens, in order, each on the one before:

## ① Self — the self-model, by CAZ modulation

A body recovers its **own connectivity graph** from movement alone. Each zone
correlates its own efference with what it hears of the rest of the body; the union
of local reads *is* the self-model, with no central reader (Commitment C3). The
key result is **topology-invariance**: the *same* framework function
([`transfer`/`coupling`](self-model-and-measurement.md)) constructs the self-model
whatever the body's shape —

- a **linear chain** ([the read-out](experiments/self_model_topology.md)),
- a **branched tree** ([branched body](experiments/branched_self_model.md)),
- and **linked chains forming a sheet and a tube**
  ([sheet & tube](experiments/lattice_self_model.md): recovery 1.00 / 0.99 / 0.97).

Irrespective of the body's complexity, one mechanism builds the self. The injected
code on each page is *byte-identical* — the invariance is not asserted, it is shown.

## ② World — a world-model from *modulated* transducers

With a self-model in hand, the **world** is world-geometry expressed in the self's
own frame. The critical word is *modulated*: the extra inputs come from the same
single-interface transducers, gated by the zones. The
[reafference cut](experiments/q2_reafference.md) separates self-caused from
world-caused change; [W1](experiments/world_in_self_graph.md) places a world feature
on the self-graph; [W2](experiments/world_geometry_self_frame.md) reads relations in
self-units; and [Q1](experiments/sweep_q1_modulation.md) shows the world-model is
built by **modulation**, not by transduction alone.

## ③ Object — aboutness as resistance

An **object** is first met as *resistance to the body's own modulation*: the action
reaches for the world and is halted by what does not yield.
[C1](experiments/c1_touch.md) shows it in the crawler (contact halts the wave);
[E1](experiments/p4_manipulator_objecthood.md) and
[E3](experiments/p6_haltability_aboutness.md) show, in a manipulator, that
**haltability generates aboutness** — object-directedness as a physical event, not a
mental posit.

---

**The Phase I pitch.** Self, world, and object are grounded — not defined into
existence — by a body doing physics. That is the concrete case for CS+physics
collaboration: the tokens cognitive science trades in acquire specific meaning only
when a physical construction fixes them.

**And then Phase I turns on itself.** Its own flagship
[null result](experiments/sweep_geometry_worldmodel.md) — adding transducers in a
line does *not* enrich the world-model — is the argument that opens
[Phase II](phase2.md).

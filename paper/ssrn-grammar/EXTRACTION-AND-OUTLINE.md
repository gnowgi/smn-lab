## The extracted core argument (spine of the preprint)

1. **The hierarchy of action patterns (the vocabulary).**
   - **FAP** — fixed, endogenous rhythm (heartbeat, gait); the metabolic canvas; *non-HAP* (cannot recompose).
   - **HAP** — haltable: interrupt / segment / recompose. *Halting introduces temporal gaps — the precondition for tokenisation.* The unit that makes both tool use and language possible.
   - **SAP** — saturated: all argument positions filled (agent/action/object co-present); index; direct engagement.
   - **USAP** — unsaturated: object slot empty; the gesture *mimics the affordance* (mime of holding a cup); first step of abstraction; Frege's unsaturated proposition / Quine's unbound variable; **iconic**.
   - **AUSAP** — arbitrary unsaturated: the iconic gesture replaced by an arbitrary token ("cup"); purely conventional; **symbol**.
   - **NAP** — negotiable: a HAP tuned in real time through interaction with world and other agents; makes social coordination possible.
   - **TAP** — transactional: performed *between* agents, leaves a *public trace*; the unit of the memetat (conventions, norms, institutions); the social form of the HAP.
   - The arc **SAP→USAP→AUSAP = Peirce's index→icon→symbol**, realized in the body's action repertoire; also habitat→memetat.

2. **Tokenization (halting → discreteness).** A continuous holistic snapshot has no tokens; HAP halting segments the stream into discrete, re-paced units — the precondition for compositional reuse and for reference.

3. **The stream and Chomsky reframed.** The snapshot is the ontological baseline (meaning constituted in coupling); the **stream** is a *transmission protocol* for inducing, in a receiver who does not share one's perceptual field, something close enough to one's own snapshot to coordinate action. Generative grammar is then **the compression/decompression protocol** — packing a high-dimensional relational structure into a 1-D sequence and unpacking it reliably. *Its domain is the interface, not the meaning.*

4. **eCAZ emancipation = the productivity step (Transition III).** Halting yields gaps but not productivity. Generativity requires **non-monotonous recomposition**: CAZs *emancipated* from fixed antagonistic pairing, supporting **switching** and **alternative pairing** across effectors → combinatorial, recursive, hierarchical, context-sensitive "motor syntax." Piggybacks on affordance-competition dynamics (Cisek; Pezzulo & Cisek); becomes distinctively human via cortical expansion + cortico-subcortical rewiring. Three composition modes: iterative, **recursive (HAP-in-HAP nesting)**, alternating.

5. **The natural/artificial inversion (closing contrast).** Natural cognition builds *upward* (habitat→memetat); artificial cognition (LLMs) builds *downward* (memetat→simulated habitat), from a frozen snapshot lacking the five living processes. Grammar in the body is grounded; positional meaning in the machine is ungrounded — the conceptual-spaces bridge (Gärdenfors) sits between.

## Proposed paper outline (~target-article length)

1. **Introduction** — the productivity/grounding dilemma; thesis: grammar is grounded in the recombinable dynamics of action patterns, and is an interface-engineering protocol, not a disembodied symbol system.
2. **The action-pattern hierarchy** — FAP/HAP/SAP/USAP/AUSAP/NAP/TAP; the semiotic arc (index→icon→symbol).
3. **Halting and tokenization** — how HAP gaps produce discreteness and reference (USAP/AUSAP; Frege/Peirce).
4. **Emancipated CAZs and recursive composition** — the productivity step; switching + alternative pairing; the three composition modes; motor syntax.
5. **The stream as transmission protocol** — snapshot→stream; Chomsky's grammar as compression/decompression of the interface (the dedicated p210 material).
6. **Affinities** — conceptual spaces (grounded→positional bridge); affordance-competition; Lashley's serial order; brief, respectful.
7. **Scope, predictions, and the road to demonstration** — what is grounded (function/substrate/discreteness/recursion-as-recomposition) vs what is reframed (grammar's domain); the smn-lab NAP experiments as the future computational demonstration; relation to the Frontiers paper.

## Likely gaps to shore up (for GN/DK to judge on reading)

1. **From motor recursion to syntactic recursion.** The recursive-composition mode (HAP-in-HAP nesting) is argued by analogy with syntactic recursion. The pressable gap: a tighter argument (or a model) that eCAZ recomposition yields the *specific formal properties* linguists mean by recursion/Merge (unbounded hierarchical embedding, displacement) rather than recursion in a loose sense. Reviewers from linguistics will press exactly here.
2. **Scope of the grammar claim.** The account grounds grammar's *function, substrate, discreteness, and combinatoriality* and *reframes its domain* (interface, not meaning); it does not derive specific syntactic universals. The preprint should state this scope explicitly to avoid over-claiming.
3. **What "emancipation" is, precisely.** eCAZ is described qualitatively (cortical expansion, rewiring, freedom from fixed pairing). A crisper architectural definition of "emancipated from fixed antagonistic pairing" — and what changes formally in the SMN tuple — would strengthen it.
4. **Demonstration is deferred.** Both papers defer the computational demonstration (smn-lab NAP/CAZ-switching experiments). Be explicit that the SSRN paper is the theoretical statement and the demonstration is forthcoming, so the pair is not read as claiming more than it shows.
5. **Deep-read still pending:** pp 130–134 and pp 210+ (the dedicated Chomsky/stream-interface section) — extract before drafting §5.
